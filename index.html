The Problems We're Actually Solving

Six structural gaps that AI can address — if we deploy it correctly.

154,000 Dormant Accounts

Prospect and former customer accounts untouched in 90+ days. These are exactly the accounts our new land SKUs were built for — yet there's almost no incentive structure to work them.

EX & MR Coverage Gaps

Smaller deal sizes and higher competition mean SDRs deprioritize these products. Price is the #1 loss reason in sub-$100K deals — doubling YoY. We built the products to compete. Now we need the coverage model.

Automated Sequences Sound Canned

Our current cadences rely on static templates that can't reference what's happening in the account today. Prospects can tell. The messaging reads as mass-produced because it is, and reply rates reflect that.

Quota Structure Drives Behavior

A $5K–$20K land deal doesn't move an individual rep's number. Valid, but thousands of accounts go unworked as a result and the business needs these deals.

Same Resources, Growing Targets

Pipeline goals are rising exponentially. Requires exponentially more quality activity. Efficiency can help here, but a human only has so much time. We need leverage that doesn't require adding headcount.

Our Motion Stops at 5pm

The outbound engine runs 8–10 hours a day, M–F. Prospects don't stop. And SDR time is buried in manual work: research, contact finding, CRM entry, sequence enrollment. Every hour on admin is an hour off the phone.

Qualify the Signal SDR + AE

Not every signal is worth acting on. Quickly assess: Is this a priority account? Does the signal map to one of our product plays? Is there a clear pain or trigger to reference?

Quick Qualification Check:
  • Account is on your target list or in your book of business
  • Signal maps to a CX, EX, or S&R use case
  • Signal is recent (< 2 weeks ideal, < 30 days max)
  • You can tie it to a specific persona and pain point

◆ Signal Type — Route Accordingly

🔄 Job Change

New hire or promotion into target persona role. Check LinkedIn profile → confirm title, scope, reports to. Best window: first 90 days — new leaders are evaluating vendors and setting priorities.

💰 Funding / Earnings

New capital = new budget. Pull the earnings call transcript or press release. Look for quotes mentioning customer experience, employee engagement, research, or digital transformation. Reference specific language in outreach.

📝 Publication / Talk

Target persona published an article, gave a talk, or posted on LinkedIn about a topic our platform solves. This is the highest-conversion signal — they're publicly stating their priorities. Reference their specific content.

📰 Company News

Expansion, M&A, restructuring, new product launch, or market entry. Map the news to the product play it unlocks. "Expanding into APAC" → CX in new markets. "Acquiring X" → EX integration challenges.

⚠ Pain Signal

Negative press, Glassdoor drops, regulatory issues, public complaints. Handle with care — don't lead with the negative. Frame around "peers solving similar challenges" and industry benchmarking.

Craft Signal-Specific Messaging SDR + AE

The outreach must reference the signal directly — this is what differentiates signal-based from generic prospecting. Use AI to draft, but always personalize the signal reference yourself.

💡 Signal Messaging Framework

Hook: Reference the specific signal (congrats on new role / saw your article on X / noticed the funding round).
Bridge: Connect it to a relevant challenge peers face in that moment.
Value: Keep the solution vague — diagnose don't prescribe. "Helping FSI leaders benchmark against peers" beats product pitching.
CTA: Low-pressure — "worth a 15-min conversation?" not "let me show you a demo."

💡 What to Include in Your AI Prompt

Contact context: Role, tenure, what they've posted or published about.
Account signal: The specific "why now" — the news, job change, funding, pain signal, or initiative you found.
Product play: Which SKU and sales play this maps to (CX, EX, S&R).
Tone: Peer-level, consultative. Diagnose the problem — don't pitch the product.
CTA: Low-pressure. "Worth a conversation?" not "Let me show you a demo."

⚠ Never Send AI Output Blindly

  • Adjust tone to sound like you, not a robot
  • If you can't find a warm signal, don't force one — fall back to persona-level pain

📋 How Mining Works — Quarter Rhythm

  • Mine each account throughout the quarter for specific products. By end of quarter, you'll have a portfolio of prospects you've reached out to — and some in your back pocket.
  • Mine by company and product. Example: Mining Visa for EX → filter by company, search for HR titles only, save to a unique list. Want CX Retention too? Filter by company, search for CX titles, save to a separate list.
  • Next quarter: Target accounts you didn't get a chance to hit and re-mine priority accounts to see if anyone new has started.
  • Result: A portfolio of plays and accounts ready for rapid initial outreach. SDRs feed AEs with qualified meetings; AEs multi-thread into their book using the same motion.

🔧 Platform Requirements for Ideal State

  • End-to-end workflow automation — from white space analysis through sequence enrollment. Not a point solution for one step.
  • Supports both SDR and AE motions — new-logo prospecting, cross-sell, multi-threading, deal acceleration. Role-aware messaging and sequences.
  • CRM integration (Salesforce) — bidirectional sync for contacts, accounts, opportunities, and activity logging.
  • Sales engagement integration (Outreach) — push contacts into sequences with pre-drafted messaging, not just into the CRM.
  • Trainable messaging model — must support fine-tuning on team-specific data: winning emails, rep edits, performance metrics, and approved playbooks.
  • Human-in-the-loop approval — nothing sends without rep review. Approve, edit, or reject at every touchpoint.
  • Signal research engine — automated scanning across news, earnings, LinkedIn, job changes, funding, and regulatory sources.
  • Contact enrichment — ZoomInfo, LinkedIn, or equivalent for verified contact data and org chart mapping.
  • Manager visibility & controls — guardrails on messaging, performance dashboards, and the ability to audit agent decisions.
  • Short-term contract — 1 year or less given the pace of AI innovation in this space.
Foundation Layer

The Real Unlock is Qualtrics Context

Any AI tool we buy is only as good as the data it pulls from. The goldmine is already inside our systems.

Generic LLMs generate generic output. The difference between a throwaway AI tool and one reps actually trust is whether the messaging sounds like it came from a Qualtrics rep who knows what wins — not a bot scraping the internet. Everything needed to train that already lives in Salesforce, Kaia, and our internal Ask AI tool. We just need to pull it into one place and feed it to whatever agent layer we land on.

Agent Output Quality = Context Quality
SFDC

Salesforce: Accounts, Activity, Opportunities

The agent reads from Accounts (industry, segment, spend, owner), Activity History (every email, call, meeting log), Historical Opportunities (open, won, lost — including won/lost reasons and primary competitor), Last Activity Date (powering dormancy detection), and Products Owned (cross-sell vs. new-logo). Every email thread, meeting note, and deal note is a record of what actually worked. Pulling this into the agent's context means outbound is grounded in how our team really talks to prospects — not scraped generic copy.

Kaia

Every discovery call, demo, and customer conversation captured in Outreach Kaia. The exact language top reps use when they're closing deals — and the questions, objections, and reactions from prospects — become training data for the agent. Battle cards stop being static PDFs and start being living context.

Closed-Won

Which accounts actually bought Survey Essentials or People Engage? What problem did they have? What messaging resonated? Closed-won opportunities are the highest-signal training data we have, and it's all sitting in SFDC already.

Ask AI

Internal Knowledge & Approved Messaging

Engineering already built an Ask AI tool that plugs into Highspot, support docs, and other internal context. Sales plays, competitive one-pagers, pricing explainers, objection responses, product documentation. Marketing

Outreach

Which subject lines got opened. Which first lines got replies. Which sequence steps converted to meetings. The historical sequencing data is a map of what the agent should emulate and what to stop doing.

CRM Layer — Salesforce Write-Back

Everything lands where it needs to

Enriched records flow back into SFDC: account creation, contact creation, territory routing, owner assignment, and a full audit trail of where the data came from. No manual CRM entry. No duplicate records.

▼
05

Sequencing Layer — Outreach + AI Messaging

Where the agent-generated messaging actually lives

Prospects are enrolled in the appropriate Outreach sequence. Every email in that sequence is AI-generated uniquely for each prospect — drawing from persona, account signals, recent news, and historical winning patterns from the context engine. Not a template with a first-name merge field. Actually personalized.

▼
06

Execution Layer — Human or Auto

Two modes depending on the rep's preference and account tier

The rep chooses how much control they want. High-value accounts get reviewed. Long-tail volume can run on autopilot with audit logs.

👤

Human-in-the-Loop

Rep reviews and approves each send. Higher quality, slower throughput. Default for Strategic and Tier 1 accounts.

🤖

Fully Automated

System runs without intervention. Full audit logs preserved. Default for cold targets and long-tail TAM where volume matters more than perfection.
